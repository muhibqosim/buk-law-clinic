THE CLINIC Activities

In actualising the objectives of clinical education, Bayero University Law Clinic (BULC) periodically carries out the following activities. Browse our activities and get abreast of what the BULC stands for.

Street Lawyering

Street Lawyering

Bayero University Law Clinic engages in impactful Street Lawyering initiatives, aiming to bridge legal awareness and societal challenges. Through this endeavor, the clinic organizes public campaigns and enlightenments on pressing issues like sexual harassment, human trafficking, drug abuse, violence, conflict, and pre-trial detention.

By bringing legal knowledge to the public sphere, the clinic actively contributes to fostering a more informed and empowered community, promoting social justice, and addressing critical societal concerns.

Visitations & Excursions

Visitation & Excursion

The Bayero University Law Clinic enhances the practical education of its students through organized visitations and excursions. These activities provide students with firsthand exposure to legal practices, institutions, and real-world scenarios.

By facilitating visits to correctional centers, detention facilities, and other relevant establishments, the clinic ensures that students gain valuable insights into the legal system while fostering a sense of social responsibility. This hands-on approach to legal education enables students to apply theoretical knowledge in practical settings, contributing to their overall professional development.

Access to Justice

Access to Justice

Bayero University Law Clinic is dedicated to ensuring Access to Justice for indigent individuals. Through pro-bono legal services, the clinic assists those awaiting trial in various correctional and detention centers, emphasizing the importance of a fair and just society. By extending legal support to the less privileged, the clinic actively contributes to breaking barriers that hinder access to justice, fostering a legal system that is inclusive and responsive to the needs of vulnerable populations. This commitment reflects the clinic's overarching goal of building a community where everyone can exercise their rights, irrespective of their socio-economic background.

Community Outreaches

Community Outreaches

Community outreach is a core activity of the Bayero University Law Clinic, designed to create a positive impact in its host community and beyond. Through educational and sensitization programs, the clinic informs constituents in Kano State about their fundamental rights, environmental concerns, domestic rights, and social and co-relative rights. These initiatives not only empower individuals with legal knowledge but also foster a broader community that is socially conscious and responsible. By actively engaging with the community, the clinic plays a vital role in promoting legal awareness, social justice, and ethical responsibility, contributing to the development of a more informed and empowered society.

Environmental Justice

Environmental Justice

Environmental justice is a focal point for Bayero University Law Clinic, reflecting its commitment to addressing ecological concerns through legal avenues. The clinic actively engages in initiatives aimed at educating and sensitizing the community on environmental rights. By advocating for a sustainable and just environment, the clinic contributes to building awareness about the legal dimensions of environmental issues. Through legal advice, representation, and advocacy, the clinic endeavors to ensure that individuals and communities have the necessary tools to protect their environmental rights. This underscores the clinic's role in promoting a harmonious balance between legal principles and environmental stewardship.

Pre-trial Detention

Pre-trial Detention

Bayero University Law Clinic addresses the critical issue of pre-trial detention through its dedicated initiatives. The clinic provides pro-bono legal services to indigent individuals awaiting trial in various correctional and detention centers. By offering legal advice, representation, and support, the clinic strives to ensure fair treatment and access to justice for those in pre-trial detention. This activity reflects the clinic's commitment to upholding the principles of justice and advocating for the rights of individuals caught in the legal process, contributing to the pursuit of a more equitable and just society.

Client Counseling and Referrals

Client Counseling and Referrals

Client counseling and referrals are integral components of Bayero University Law Clinic's commitment to providing comprehensive legal support. The clinic, under the supervision of experienced legal professionals, actively engages in counseling sessions with clients. Through empathetic and informed guidance, the clinic assists individuals in understanding their legal rights and navigating their legal challenges. Furthermore, the clinic facilitates appropriate referrals when necessary, ensuring that clients receive specialized assistance beyond the clinic's scope.

This personalized approach underscores the clinic's dedication to not only offering direct legal aid but also fostering holistic support for individuals seeking assistance in the complex legal landscape.

Court Case Monitoring

Court Case Monitoring

Bayero University Law Clinic demonstrates its commitment to legal advocacy through court case monitoring. This activity involves closely tracking legal proceedings to ensure transparency and fairness. By monitoring court cases, the clinic aims to safeguard the rights of individuals involved, particularly those who may lack resources or face challenges in navigating the legal system.

This proactive approach aligns with the clinic's broader mission of promoting access to justice and contributing to a legal environment characterized by accountability and equity. Through diligent court case monitoring, the clinic actively contributes to the pursuit of a just and equitable legal system.

The Bayero University Law Clinc (BULC) is a Law Clinic established in Faculty of Law, Bayero University, Kano in the year 2017 and duly registered under the Network of Universities Legal Aid Institution (NULAI). It is an avenue that gives law students an opportunity to know the practical aspect of law through mechanisms of Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR), Social Justice and Access to Justice. It also educates, trains and sensitizes students, staff, and communities around the Bayero University Campus on their fundamental rights, social rights, domestic rights, career development and co-relative rights. The Bayero University Law Clinic offers pro-bono (free) legal service in form of legal advice, representation, dispute resolution, advocacy and sensitization to indigent persons.
